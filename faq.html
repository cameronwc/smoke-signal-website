01 / The Basics

What Smoke Signal Is

1.1What is Smoke Signal?

Smoke Signal is an off-grid communication and navigation app for iPhone and iPad. It gives your group encrypted chat, live location sharing on a shared map, offline maps, route recording, and emergency SOS — all over a LoRa mesh radio, with no cell service, no internet, and no accounts.

1.2Who is it for?

Groups that go where coverage doesn't. Hikers and backpackers, backcountry skiers, hunters, paddlers, search-and-rescue teams, and crews on remote work sites. If your group splits up beyond shouting distance and beyond cell bars, Smoke Signal keeps everyone on the same map and in the same conversation.

1.3Does it really work without cell service?

Yes — that's the entire point. Smoke Signal is built to operate with zero cell coverage and zero internet. Messages, locations, and SOS alerts travel directly between devices over radio. Nothing depends on a tower, a satellite, or a server.

02 / The Mesh

How Off-Grid Messaging Works

2.1How does messaging work without the internet?

Your phone pairs with a small LoRa mesh radio over Bluetooth. The radios talk to each other directly over long-range, low-power radio waves — think of each one as a tiny relay tower your group carries.

Here's the part that matters: every radio in the group also repeats messages it hears. If you're too far from a friend to reach them directly, your message hops through the radios in between until it arrives. More people in your group means more relays, more range, and more paths for a message to get through. If one path drops, the mesh routes around it.

2.2What hardware do I need?

Two things: an iPhone or iPad running iOS 18.2 or later, and a compatible LoRa mesh radio for each group member who needs long range. The radio pairs with your phone over Bluetooth and rides in a pocket or clips to a pack strap.

No radio yet? At close range, Smoke Signal phones can also chat directly with each other over local peer-to-peer wireless — useful around camp, but the radio is what carries your group across valleys.

2.3Which radios work with Smoke Signal?

Smoke Signal pairs with compatible LoRa mesh radios over Bluetooth — including the Smoke Signal radio we're building, designed end-to-end for the app. Pair it once and it just works: GPS positions, messages, and battery telemetry flow straight into the app.

Join the launch list to hear when the app and radio ship.

2.4What range can I expect?

Honest answer: it depends on terrain. Radio range is governed by line of sight. In dense forest or rolling hills, plan on roughly a kilometre or two per hop. From a ridgeline or across open water, a single hop can stretch many times that.

Remember that range compounds — every member's radio relays messages, so a spread-out group covers far more ground than any single link. Treat any fixed number with suspicion, including ours: test with your group before you depend on it.

2.5How long do the batteries last?

The radio runs on its own battery and is built to sip power — days of standby are typical. Cold weather, frequent position broadcasts, and heavy message traffic all cut into that, so pack a small battery bank on multi-day trips.

On your phone, the app is optimised for efficient background operation, and you can watch every group member's radio battery level right in the app — no guessing who's about to go dark.

03 / Maps & Navigation

Offline Maps and Routes

3.1How do offline maps work?

Download your maps before you leave coverage. In the app, pick a region, set a radius, choose the detail level, and Smoke Signal caches the map tiles on your device — best done on Wi-Fi, since a large region is a big download.

Once cached, the map works anywhere. GPS doesn't need cell service, so your position, your group's positions, and your breadcrumb trail all keep rendering on the stored map with zero connectivity.

3.2Can I plan and record routes?

Yes. Record live routes with real-time distance, elapsed time, and elevation stats. Import GPX files from your planning tools, export your recordings to share with the group, and drop waypoints with coordinates and elevation as you move.

04 / Groups, Privacy & SOS

Your Group, Your Data

4.1How do group chats and channels work?

Your group shares a channel — a name plus an encryption key. Everyone on the channel sees group messages and appears on the shared map. Sharing a channel takes seconds: one person shows a QR code, everyone else scans it. You can also message individual group members directly.

4.2Who can read my messages?

Only people who hold your channel key. Messages over the mesh are encrypted with that key — another radio in the area without it just hears noise. Phone-to-phone messages are encrypted end-to-end as well, and the key itself is stored in your device's secure Keychain, never in plain storage.

4.3How does the SOS feature work?

Trigger SOS and your exact GPS coordinates broadcast to every member of your group, re-sending automatically until you cancel. Everyone gets an alert and sees your position on their map, so the people closest to you can move first. A countdown before broadcast prevents false alarms.

4.4Does SOS contact rescue services?

No — and you should know this before you rely on it. Smoke Signal's SOS reaches your group, over the mesh, within radio range. It does not contact emergency services, and it is not a satellite communicator. For solo travel far from any partner, carry a dedicated satellite device. Smoke Signal is built for group coordination — the fastest help is usually the person one ridge over.

4.5What data does Smoke Signal collect about me?

None. There are no accounts to create, no cloud to sync to, no analytics, and no tracking. Your messages, locations, routes, and SOS history live on your device and travel only to your group over local radio. We never see them — there's no server to send them to.

You can delete chat history, location history, or everything at once from the app's settings. Full details in our Privacy Policy.

05 / Pricing

What It Costs

5.1Is there a subscription?

No. Never. Satellite communicators charge monthly fees because satellite airtime costs money. LoRa mesh radio costs nothing to use — so we have nothing to charge you for each month. No subscription, no activation fees, no airtime plans.

5.2So what do I actually pay for?

Two one-time purchases: the radio hardware, and a one-time unlock in the app for the full feature set. Buy it once, own it outright — like the rest of your gear. The app downloads free, so you can pair a radio and try the core experience before unlocking anything.
